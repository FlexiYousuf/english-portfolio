Lesson 1.2: Bias and Belonging

Article Analysis: "The Skin I’m In" by Desmond Cole

1. Examples of Cognitive Bias

In the article, we see cognitive biases at play when people in authority make quick, flawed mental shortcuts about Desmond Cole based entirely on his race.

Personal Connection

I have noticed how easily our brains rely on these shortcuts in daily life. For example, I used to judge people by their clothes and elegance, assuming they were automatically smart or possessed strong leadership skills, but after a while, I realized that I was wrong. This is a cognitive bias known as the Halo Effect, where we let one positive trait trick us into assuming everything else about a person is perfect. Another example is how I used to listen to crowd opinions; many people claimed a certain video game was bad and boring, but when I finally tried it myself, it became clear to me that it was one of the best fictional experiences I had ever engaged with. This shows how cognitive bias can blind us to reality until we experience it firsthand.

2. Unconscious Bias and Stereotypes

The article is filled with examples of unconscious bias and dangerous stereotypes, specifically from the police who continuously pulled over, questioned, or interrogated Cole simply because he is Black. They automatically associated his skin color with suspicion or criminality without any actual evidence. This represents how unconscious bias operates automatically and is deeply rooted in societal stereotypes.

Personal Connection

I experienced a form of visual judgment and stereotyping myself when I traveled outside my country. While eating at a fancy restaurant, I noticed that some people were looking at me with disapproval or judgment. They formed an immediate opinion about me based purely on my appearance or the fact that I was an outsider, showing how quickly people let unconscious biases dictate how they treat strangers.

3. Impact on the Individual and Sense of Belonging

These continuous experiences with bias had a massive negative impact on Desmond Cole. Being interrogated over 50 times stripped away his sense of belonging. Instead of feeling like a safe, equal citizen in his own community, the constant profiling made him feel like a perpetual suspect and an outsider in his own home. It shows that when society constantly views you through a lens of bias, it makes it incredibly difficult to feel comfortable or accepted.

Personal Reflection on Bias

1. How biases influence my thinking

Biases force my brain to make quick, automatic assumptions based on surface-level details instead of taking the time to look deeper.

2. Inaccurate judgments I have made

As shown in my examples above, biases easily lead me to wrong conclusions. They make me jump to conclusions based on outward appearances or popular opinions before I actually get to know a person or try something myself.

3. How the biases of others impacted me

When others judge me based on their own biases, it feels incredibly isolating. Being stared at or looked down upon based on an assumption makes me feel uncomfortable, misjudged, and completely out of place.

4. Next steps to counteract bias

I need to slow down my thinking and give people or things a real chance before forming a final opinion. Challenging my first impressions and staying open-minded is the best way to make sure I don't let shortcuts ruin a great experience or misjudge a good person.
